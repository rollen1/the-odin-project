Cepelinai

Description

Cepelinai (pronounced sep-elle-in-ay) are a delicious type of potato and meat dumpling from Lithuanian. They get their name due to their zeppelin-like shape (cepelinai is zeppelin in Lithuanian). Made with mouthwateringly savory meat filling wrapped in tender potato dough, cepelinai are typically served with a delicious gravy, crumbled bacon, and sour cream. They were originally known as didžkukuliai, but were renamed for Count Ferdinand von Zeppelin.

Ingredients

Potato dough

  • 2/1 pounds potatoes
  • teaspon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of dry potato starch

Filling

  • 2/3 pound ground pork, beef, chicken or turkey (or combo)
  • 1 medium onion
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley and dill to taste

Topping/sauce

  • 1/4 pound bacon, fried crisp then crumbled and set aside
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • Butter
  • Bacon grease or oil
  • Sour cream
  • Chopped fresh dill and parsley, to taste, if desired

Steps

  1. Boil 1/3 of the potatoes for 15 minutes, or until tender.
  2. Drain, and allow the potatoes to cool. Peel, then mash and set aside.
  3. In a bowl, combine the meat, onion, salt, pepper, parsley, and dill. Cover and place in the fridge.
  4. Peel the remaining 2/3 of the potatoes. Finely grate them with a box grater.
  5. Place the potatoes onto a clean paper towel.
  6. Squeeze the juices into a bowl, and set aside for about 5 minutes so the heavier starches can settle to the bottom of the bowl.
  7. In a second bowl, combine the mashed potatoes, grated potatoes, salt, and dry potato starch.
  8. Discard the top layer of watery potato juice. Add the sediment at the bottom of the bowl to the potatoes. Mix well.
  9. Boil salted water in a large pot.
  10. Divide the potato dough into 6 equal parts. Form one piece into a flat disc.
  11. Place a tablespoon of the filling in the center. Fold the disk over and crimp the edges to seal the dumpling. Repeat with remaining dough.
  12. Working in batches, place the dumplings in the boiling water.
  13. Cook for 15 to 20 minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove with a slotted spoon.
  14. To make the sauce, sauté the onions in butter and 2 tablespoons of bacon grease.
  15. Season with salt and pepper. Cook for 5 to 7 minutes, until golden.
  16. Pour the sauce over the dumplings. Add a spoonful of sour cream and garnish with crumbled bacon.
